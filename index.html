Patient & Visitor's Guide

For Out-patient Approval — Consultation

1. Initial Consultation

  1. Proceed to the Information/Lobby for proper directions to the doctors' offices. Ask the information staff which type of doctor is suitable for your illness.
  2. Proceed to the HMO Department on the 3rd floor and present one valid ID and health card to the HMO staff.
  3. The HMO staff will contact the HMO company to verify the member's status and eligibility.
  4. Once approved, the patient will sign the approved Letter of Authorization (LOA), then proceed to the doctor's clinic.
  5. Give the LOA to the doctor for signing.

2. Follow-up Consultation

  1. Direct to the HMO Department on the 3rd floor, then present one valid ID and health card to the HMO staff.
  2. The HMO staff will contact the HMO company to verify the member's status and eligibility.
  3. Once approved, the patient will sign the approved Letter of Authorization (LOA), then proceed to the doctor's clinic.
  4. Give the LOA to the doctor for signing.

With Procedure and Laboratory Request

  1. Direct to the HMO Department on the 3rd floor, then present one valid ID and health card to the HMO staff.
  2. The HMO staff will contact the HMO company to verify the member's status and eligibility.
  3. Once approved, the patient will sign the approved Letter of Authorization (LOA), then proceed to the doctor's clinic.
  4. Proceed to the designated department (Laboratory, Radiology, Heart Station, OB, Pulmo, etc.) and present the approved LOA to the hospital staff.

Direct Admission

Refers to an approved patient for direct room admission without passing through the Emergency Room of Bicutan Medical Center, Inc., with medically necessary and appropriate care and treatment for illness or injury.

  1. Admitting Order — an order coming from the doctor that initiates the direct admission process. The date of the admitting order should be the same as the date of admission.
  2. Before admission, our admitting staff will request the following:
    • Doctor's order for admission indicating the directives of the personal physician regarding the patient's case.
    • Valid Identification Card with photo, such as:
      • Accredited HMO-issued member's ID
      • Digitized SSS ID
      • Driver's License
      • GSIS ID
      • Health insurance card or document
      • Integrated Bar of the Philippines ID
      • Passport
      • Person with Disability ID
      • PhilHealth ID
      • PRC ID
      • Senior Citizen ID
      • Unified Multi-Purpose ID (UMID)
    • HMO or Insurance Card
    • Letter of Guarantee or Letter of Authorization (LOA) from the company or HMO Coordinator
  3. Fill out and sign the forms necessary for the admission process.
  4. Admitting staff will explain the policies of the hospital and what is indicated in the consent form.

Visitors are an important part of the healing process, and we encourage family and friends to visit. However, rest is very important to a patient — so visiting hours and rules may vary by ward/unit, especially in Intensive Care Units.

1. Visiting Time

Non-Critical Areas (GNU / Wards)

9:00 AM – 11:00 AM

5:00 PM – 8:00 PM

Adult & Pediatric ICU

11:00 AM – 12:00 PM

6:00 PM – 7:00 PM

Neonatal ICU (NICU)

9:00 AM – 11:00 AM

5:00 PM – 7:00 PM

2. Age of Visitors

Children below 7 years old are not allowed. However, if there is a request from the patient, they may be allowed provided there is proper coordination with the charge nurse/supervisor on duty and the guard on duty. Children should be primed by the family on what to expect and must be supervised at all times by a responsible adult who is not the patient.

3. Visitors' Health

Any visitor exposed to or having a communicable disease (tuberculosis, measles, mumps, rubella, pertussis, flu, or a cold/cough) should not visit patients.

4. Number of Visitors

It is usually best to limit visitors to two (2) at a time, to respect the comfort of all our patients.

5. General Information

  • Staff members are happy to support the time you and your visitors have together. At times during your stay, they may ask visitors to leave for a short period while care is delivered or during some medical procedures.
  • Visitors should respect the privacy of others, stay quiet, and remain in the room they are visiting or in the waiting room. We aim to provide a calm and restful environment to help all patients recover.
  • Visitors and watchers are requested to refrain from making noise. Gambling is absolutely prohibited in the hospital.
  • Visitors should not sit or lie on patient beds, to prevent cross-infection.
  • Visitors must wear IDs at all times. IDs are available at the Security Guard in the Hospital Lobby.
  • Only one (1) watcher is allowed per patient. Watchers should secure a Watcher's Pass from the Security Guard at the Hospital Lobby.
  • Bringing of sleeping paraphernalia is not allowed for infection-control purposes; however, the patient's watcher may borrow linen or a blanket from the nursing unit.

6. Smoking / Drinking Alcoholic Beverages

Smoking and drinking alcoholic beverages are strictly prohibited in all areas of the hospital.

7. Use of Cellular Phones

The use of cellular phones is prohibited where critical medical equipment is in operation and within six feet of any electronic patient-care equipment. If you must use your cellular phone, please speak in a low voice so as not to disturb others. Cellphone charging is strictly prohibited.

8. Restricted Flower Deliveries

Flowers for patients are welcome, but there are some areas where flowers cannot be permitted under any circumstances — especially in intensive care units, where they may promote infection. We encourage you to check with the nursing staff before sending or bringing flowers.

9. Bringing of Firearms

For security reasons, all firearms must be deposited at the Security Guard before entering hospital premises.

10. Bringing of Latex Balloons

Latex balloons, which can cause allergic reactions, are not permitted anywhere in the hospital.

11. Special Considerations

A religious group may be allowed to pray over a patient, provided that the patient's status is stable and there is no ongoing procedure at the bedside. The guard should coordinate with the head nurse/charge nurse of the unit and the NSOD. However, the number of persons doing the pray-over should be limited because of limited space.

Visiting Guidelines (NICU)

1. Viewing Time for NICU / Outborn

NICU / Outborn

9:00 AM – 11:00 AM

5:00 PM – 7:00 PM

  1. Only parents of critically ill neonates may be allowed to enter the NICU/Outborn anytime, with the permission of the Attending Physician.
  2. Visitors, except children, may enter the NICU/Outborn one at a time and shall strictly observe hand washing and the no-jewelry policy, and must put on a gown and rubber slippers provided by the unit, as allowed by the Attending Physician.
  3. Cellphones are not allowed inside the NICU/Outborn.

Medical Records

Transaction Hours and Schedule of Release

The Medical Records Office accepts requests for medical records from Mondays to Fridays, 8:30 AM until 12 noon. Patients can request the following records with corresponding release schedules:

  • Birth Certificate — Within 10 working days
  • Death Certificate — 1 to 2 working days
  • Certificate of Confinement (CC) — Within the day of request
  • Examination Results — Within the day of request (if available)
  • Medical Certificate (MC) — Within 5 working days
  • Discharge Summary (DS) — Within 7 working days
  • Clinical Abstract (CA) — Within 7 working days

Claiming of Requested Results and Legal Documents

  • If the patient is claiming the results/documents, a valid ID must be presented.
  • If an authorized representative is claiming the request, an authorization letter signed by the patient, a photocopy of the patient's valid ID, and the representative's valid ID must be provided.
